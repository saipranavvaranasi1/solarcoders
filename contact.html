Common questions

Before you write in.

Is $>SolarCoders<$ really free?

Yes — completely. $>SolarCoders<$ is a nonprofit funded by donors and run by volunteers, so there's no tuition and no hidden costs. All a student needs is a computer and an internet connection.

What age and experience is this for?

Our programs are built for ages 8–14, from total beginners to kids who've tinkered before. Orbit 01 starts from zero; Orbit 03 introduces AI for students ready for a challenge. If you're unsure, pick "help me choose" on the enrollment form.

How much time does it take each week?

Crews meet once a week for about an hour, over 6–8 weeks depending on the orbit. There's no mandatory homework — just optional missions for kids who want to keep building between sessions.

Can I volunteer if I'm not a professional developer?

Absolutely. If you're comfortable with the basics of Python and enjoy working with kids, we'll train you on our curriculum and pair you with an experienced mentor for your first crew. Reach out at mentors@$>SolarCoders<$.org.

Can you run a cohort at our school or library?

We'd love to. We partner with schools, libraries, and community programs to bring crews to more kids — including in person where we can. Email partners@$>SolarCoders<$.org and we'll figure out a fit together.
